Our Opioid Response Policy

Commitment to Responsible Opioid Dispensing

At Your Pharmacy, we are committed to addressing the opioid crisis by dispensing opioid medications responsibly and adhering to all regulatory guidelines. We take an active role in ensuring that these medications are used safely and appropriately, minimizing the risk of misuse or addiction.

1. Prescription Verification and Monitoring

We closely monitor all opioid prescriptions to ensure that they are legitimate and necessary. Our pharmacists verify each prescription for accuracy and appropriateness based on the patient's medical history and condition.

  • We adhere to local and federal regulations governing the dispensing of controlled substances.
  • Our pharmacists are trained to identify signs of opioid misuse or over-prescription.
  • We maintain an electronic record of opioid prescriptions to track patient usage and prevent over-dispensing.

2. Collaboration with Healthcare Providers

We work closely with doctors, pain management specialists, and other healthcare providers to ensure that opioid medications are used as part of a broader pain management plan. Communication between our pharmacists and prescribers is essential to ensure patient safety.

3. Patient Education and Support

Our pharmacy provides comprehensive education to patients receiving opioid medications. This includes discussing the risks, safe usage, and proper storage and disposal of opioid medications.

  • We explain the potential risks of opioid dependence and addiction.
  • We provide information on non-opioid alternatives when appropriate.
  • Patients are encouraged to contact our pharmacists with any questions or concerns regarding their medications.

4. Safe Disposal of Opioid Medications

To reduce the risk of unused opioids being misused, we offer guidance on how to properly dispose of any leftover medication. We partner with local drug take-back programs and provide disposal instructions on all opioid prescriptions.

  • We offer in-store medication disposal options where available.
  • Patients are encouraged to bring back unused opioids during national take-back events or to use community disposal kiosks.

5. Preventing Opioid Misuse and Addiction

We are committed to preventing opioid misuse and addiction by engaging in public health initiatives and raising awareness about the opioid epidemic. Our pharmacy actively supports efforts to reduce opioid-related harm.

  • We provide resources for patients and their families on recognizing the signs of opioid misuse and where to seek help.
  • Our pharmacists participate in continuing education programs to stay up-to-date on the latest opioid prescribing guidelines and treatment options for opioid use disorder (OUD).

6. Opioid Overdose Prevention and Naloxone Access

We offer naloxone (Narcan) to patients at risk of opioid overdose and educate them on how to use it. Naloxone is a life-saving medication that can reverse the effects of an opioid overdose when administered promptly.

  • Naloxone is available without a prescription in certain areas. Ask our pharmacist if you or a loved one could benefit from having naloxone on hand.
  • We provide training on how to recognize an opioid overdose and administer naloxone safely.

7. Compliance with Federal and State Laws

Our pharmacy complies with all federal and state laws regarding the dispensing of opioids and controlled substances. We are registered with the Drug Enforcement Administration (DEA) and participate in Prescription Drug Monitoring Programs (PDMP) to track the dispensing of opioids and prevent abuse.
